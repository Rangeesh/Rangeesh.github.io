This thought is an aftermath of a day full of thinking. I fell I'm not ready in a lot of different things. Sometimes, it's if I'm ready for marriage. Sometimes, it is if I'm ready to meet someone and talk to them. Sometimes, it is if I'm ready to pose for pictures. 

The point is, I feel I'm never ready and it causes me disappointment. Foe eg., I'm 80 kgs now, overweight. I didn't want to play football until I could run better (at least not with a group of people). I didn't want to be ashamed of not having any stamina in front of a group of new people.

Same goes for marriage. I don't want them (or anyone new) to see my balding head. I feel ashamed of it. 

I'm also scared to start doing ML stuff at Tesla. I feel I'm not ready for it. 

BUT

I don't actively take the efforts to go there. Or it is not consistent. I don't really know where the problem is. Perhaps, I don't want it enough? Or do I not just care about it? I do know for sure, sometimes if things need to be done for the team, I get them done. But not for myself. 'Cause who am I letting down if I don't do a task I set for myself? Me. That's okay, right? *I can adjust*. 

The problem I am facing now is, nobody cares about me as a person anymore. It's just me, for myself. So, I don't have any targets set that I care about or anything for that sort. I don't have to report to anyone strict, or anything of that sort. But you know what, I do have to deal with the consequences. 

...After a few distractions and thoughts...

But here's the thing. When you lose, your mom and dad lose. All the sacrifices your parents made will be of no use. All the sacrifices you made will be of no use. 

My parents are coming here in April. I'm going to be a millionaire when I meet them in the US (well, that depends on US stocks, but hoping it doesn't go down :) ). But, at the same time, I want to be healthy and good looking when I meet them. No regrets. So, let me make a few things clear. 

I might have the memory of a goldfish at this point, but I will fight all that shit I have going in my head and get back on track. It's just a few months man, it's totally fine. 

...After a few hours...

I came across this Sunil Chhetri interview on Youtube. He says, "Do something that you said you'll do for 6 months, and see how you feel about yourself. You'll feel fucking amazing!". He's right. And that's why I feel like shit. That's why I feel I'm never ready. 'Cause I never do what I say I will do. That's needs to change. 

Note: Do what you say you will do. That is the single most powerful thing for you. And the reason you don't do it is because you didn't want it enough. Nothing else. You felt it was an additional task. You didn't see it as a task where you had to do it to survive. You scored 100s in your math test to survive. You scored insane marks in school and JEE to survive. You're not in survival mode anymore. So, you don't work hard. Nothing bad will happen to you if you don't hit the gym, so you don't work hard. 

That's why playing sports in general is increasing the feeling of disguist everytime you feel your tummy. You are carrying 8 extra kilos of fat in your body. That's so awful. 

Rangeesh, make a few promises. And stick to them no matter what. You get an holiday for 5 days from Nov 28 to Dec 3. And back to the grind right after. Deadline is Dec 24. Need to hit my year end goals by then. 

In 35 days, I will do the following:
1. Avg Phone Usage shouldn't cross 1 hour. I think I can pull this off now. 
2. I will wake up at 6 AM daily no matter what. This is going to be the hardest. It's not easy. 
3. I will not order from doordash no matter what. Already 20 days in.
4. I will not <<>> no matter what. The most important. 
5. See, I also want to figure out a way to log on MyFitnessPal daily and start tracking, but somehow I am not doing it. If I don't do 6, how will I ever become thin and lose 8 kgs? And amongst all this, I need to do minoxidil too. It's not easy. 

I'm scared of breaking the second one. It's not going to be easy. It is going to be hard. And I don't really need it to survive if you think about it. But I need to do it. I need to attach my survival to waking up at 6 AM daily. Otherwise, I won't succeed. Same goes for 4. 

Just remember this, the pain of regret is much much worse than the pain of discipline. Think about this everyday, and that's why you're going to work hard. To survive. Ronaldo did the same, so did Chhetri. 

I think I'll change the contents to "Learnings from the Sunil Chettri interview". They're really good. When you are having a bad day, relax. Don't do anything. When the good day comes, be as disciplined as possible. Go into damage control mode, and minimize damage. Otherwise, get in the groove and do everything.

I have a feeling I will forget doing what I told I will do, and suddenly one day I'll remember I told all these to myself and feel bad about it. What do I do? How do I constantly remind myself? I don't really know. Maybe my ringtone needs to have a voice recording instead? I'll try this out. 

That day is Dec 12 2024. Man I'm a total piece of shit.
